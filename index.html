Bukeikhanov, Alikhan Nurmukhamedovich

Chairman of the Government of Alash-Orda

From left to right: Zhakabai Atykeuly, Zhubandyk Bolganbaiuly, Turagul Abayuly, Alikhan Bokeikhan, Raimzhan Marsekuli, unknown; standing: unknown, Mukan Zhakezhanuly, Kokpai Zhanatayuly, unknown. Semey (then the capital of Alash Orda), 1918

Here's a time line of Bukeikhanov, Alikhan Nurmukhamedovich's life:

  • 1866 - born in Tokraunskaya volost, Karkaraly district, Semipalatinsk region, Russian Empire.
  • 1894 - He graduated from the Omsk Technical School, then entered the Faculty of Economics of the St. Petersburg Forestry Institute.
  • 1904 - Bukeikhanov participated in the expedition of F.A. Shcherbina to prepare the mass resettlement of peasants from Central Russia to the Steppe region.
  • 1906 - was arrested “as the leader of the Kyrgyz political movement.” He spent 3 months in Pavlodar prison (then 2 weeks in Omsk prison), by order of a citizen correcting the post of Governor General, Lieutenant General Romanov, was released from Omsk prison, arrived in Semipalatinsk, where he was warmly welcomed.
  • 1908 - arrested again, released from Pavlodar prison.
  • 1917 - (9 years) was in “voluntary contractual” exile in Samara, working as the chief banking agent of the Don Land Bank, and was engaged in the assessment of estates and land mortgages.
  • 1922 - 1927 - was a literary employee of the Kazakh section of the Central Publishing House of the Peoples of the USSR in Moscow.
  • 1920 - 1930 - he was arrested three times by the NKVD.
  • 1937 - Convicted by the VKVS for belonging to a “terrorist organization” and executed on the same day (together with Nurmakov).
